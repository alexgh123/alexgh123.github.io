Pairing and Feedback: A Conversation
10/20/14
< What was it like to pair with others to solve challenges?

I think pairing with another person is a great way to exchange ideas and learn new things in a very smooth way. Almost passivley.

Was something particularly fun or rewarding?

It is a very simple, but also very useful, the "cmd + /" key stroke comments out blocks of text, love that

Was something frustrating and difficult for you?

Something that is frustrating for me is spending too much time on a problem and not recognizing that is what I am doing.





How did you feel when you read your feedback?

Because of my personality, I love feedback. I imagine some people can feel very negative and self conscious about it, that is not the case for me. I love it and get disapointed if I do not think it is realistic, or sometimes even harsh enough.

Was the feedback you received helpful to your learning?

The most helpful feedback is what I can improve on. In one case, it was loops and iterations.

Based on the feedback you've received, what are you going to do to improve next time you pair?

Reading more of the well grounded Rubyist for lots of topics.

How was it to write feedback?

For me, I sometimes struggle because I can be very critical. I do not think I am judgemental, but I know I want the most negative advice, so I can get better at my weaknesses. I project that onto others, when in fact, they were really good.
